Can Tourists Use Alipay in China?

Everything international visitors need to know about mobile payments in China.

ChinaMigo Editorial Updated 2026
On this page

On this page

Overview

Mobile payments are part of daily life in China. For many visitors, Alipay is the easiest way to pay for cafes, restaurants, taxis, high-speed rail snacks, convenience stores and many local services.

The setup can feel unfamiliar at first, especially if you are used to credit cards or cash. Once it is ready, everyday movement through modern China becomes calmer, faster and easier.

“China’s mobile payment system may feel confusing at first, but once set up, it becomes surprisingly seamless.”

Can foreigners use Alipay?

Yes. International visitors can usually use Alipay by downloading the app, creating an account and linking a supported foreign bank card. Availability can vary by card issuer, country and verification flow.

Important note

Set up and test Alipay before you rely on it for airport transfers, meals or train station purchases.

What you need before setup

Keep your passport, mobile number, bank card and internet connection ready. Verification is usually simple, but having everything nearby prevents stressful setup moments after arrival.

Payment setup checklist

  • Passport ready
  • Alipay installed
  • Foreign card linked
  • Internet connection available
01

How to link a foreign card

Open Alipay, go to the card or wallet area, and add your international card. Follow the verification prompts from your bank. Some cards may require a one-time code, app approval or additional security confirmation.

ChinaMigo Tip

Always set up your payment apps before arriving at a restaurant or station.

Where Alipay works

Alipay is widely used in major cities, cafes, restaurants, convenience stores, malls, ride-hailing flows and many ticketing moments. Smaller merchants may still prefer local payment methods or cash, so keep a backup.

Common problems

The most common issues are bank verification failures, transaction limits, unstable internet, or merchants using payment flows that are not fully compatible with foreign cards.

01

Start small

Try a lower amount first if your card approval feels slow.

02

Keep a backup

Carry a second card or some cash for arrival-day moments.

If something fails, try a smaller amount, check your bank approval message, restart the app, or use a backup card. For arrival day, a little preparation prevents a lot of friction.

ChinaMigo tips

Screenshot your hotel address, keep your passport name consistent across bookings, and test one small payment as soon as your app is ready. For groups, make sure more than one person has a working payment setup.
